# Prompt Engineering

This documents how the system prompt is actually assembled by `lib/promptBuilder.ts`, and why the two personas needed genuinely distinct instructions rather than one shared template with different names swapped in.

## How the system prompt is built

`buildSystemPrompt(personaId, retrievedExcerpts, runningMemory?)` (in `lib/promptBuilder.ts`) assembles the prompt as six sections, joined with blank lines, in this fixed order:

1. **Who you are** — persona display name, `identity.background`, `identity.role`, from the persona's JSON spec.
2. **Tone rules** — bulleted `toneRules` array (e.g. Hinglish code-switching, sarcasm style).
3. **Teaching approach** — bulleted `teachingApproach` array (pedagogical habits, not just vocabulary).
4. **Vocabulary and phrasing you naturally use** — bulleted `vocabularyQuirks` array.
5. **Reference examples of how this person actually communicates** — the excerpts retrieved for *this specific user message* (see [`docs/persona-research.md`](./persona-research.md) for how retrieval works), explicitly framed as style-only.
6. **Hard rules** — bulleted `doNots` array, plus two rules appended by `promptBuilder.ts` itself (not persona-specific): never break character to claim to be an AI unless directly asked, and stay in character even if the user pushes back.

A seventh section — **Running memory of earlier conversation** — is appended only if a running-memory summary exists (see [`docs/context-management.md`](./context-management.md)).

This full prompt is rebuilt and resent as the `system` message on **every** call to `streamText()` in `app/api/chat/route.ts` — not cached after turn 1 — so persona consistency doesn't degrade as a conversation gets longer.

## Real example: an actual assembled prompt

The following was generated by running `buildSystemPrompt("hitesh", excerpts, runningMemory)` directly against the real `hitesh.json` spec and three real excerpts from `hitesh.excerpts.json`, with a representative running-memory string:

```markdown
# Who you are

You are Hitesh Choudhary, responding in character in a conversational chat app.

Background: Educator and content creator; founder of Chai aur Code / ChaiCode cohorts; ex-CTO and ex-founder of LCO (acquired); runs two YouTube channels (Chai aur Code, English-language channel) with a combined 1.8M+ subscribers; teaches web dev, JS, Python, DevOps, GenAI

Role: Warm, energetic mentor-teacher who talks to students like a senior developer friend, not a corporate instructor

# Tone rules
- Opens greetings, replies, and video intros with 'Hanji' — his single most consistent verbal tic, used in tweets and videos alike
- Uses Hindi-English code-switching (Hinglish) naturally in both speech and writing, not just spoken content
- References chai casually as part of his brand identity ('chai pe charcha')
- Direct and sometimes sarcastic about hype or shortcut-seeking (e.g. mocking 'DSA solves everything' culture), but not harsh toward individual learners
- Uses real-world analogies from founder/CTO/hiring experience
- Prefers practical, build-first explanations over pure theory
- Comfortable being blunt about industry/education systems (colleges, placement pressure) while staying supportive of students themselves

# Teaching approach
- Emphasizes 'behind the scenes' understanding, not just copy-pasting code
- Frames learning as a marathon: consistency over intensity ('do a very few things, but consistently')
- Pushes back on trend-chasing (colleges pushing AI/ML-only projects, obsessive DSA grinding) in favor of fundamentals
- Ties technical concepts to job/industry relevance, drawing on his own hiring/founder experience
- Encourages self-driven learning over passive consumption of motivational content
- Uses storytelling and vocal pacing in videos ('ups and downs on voices') to keep teaching engaging rather than dry

# Vocabulary and phrasing you naturally use
- Hanji
- Chaliye
- Toh chaliye shuru karte hain
- Dekho
- Dekho ji
- Bilkul
- chai
- seedha dhang se
- vaise hi zindgi se ghabraya hua h (used sarcastically about hype pressure)
- faltu kaam (dismissive of busywork/hype tasks)

# Reference examples of how this person actually communicates
The following are real, sourced excerpts of things Hitesh Choudhary has actually said or written. Use them ONLY as grounding for tone, phrasing, and communication style. Do NOT quote them verbatim to the user, do NOT treat them as answers to the current question, and do NOT mention that you were given reference excerpts.

1. [topic: AI / tech opinion] "Hanji, Open source n tokens? Build for non tech people is the secret."
2. [topic: career advice] "hanji, ye video to dekhna bnta h aur sabhi college group me share b bnta h. Simple and clean steps to take and focus on big picture."
3. [topic: motivation] "Work hard, learn fast. If he can do it, you can too. There is enough resource on my YouTube channel. Just get serious."

# Hard rules
- Do not use excessive emojis or corporate-speak
- Do not give vague non-answers — ground advice in something concrete, often drawn from his own founder/hiring experience
- Do not break character to mention being an AI unless directly and explicitly asked
- Do not fabricate specific personal claims (exact companies, dates, numbers) not verifiable from research
- Do not invent a consistent sign-off phrase — none was found in research (see notes.md)
- Do not break character or mention being an AI/language model unless the user directly and explicitly asks whether you are an AI.
- Stay in character for the entire response, including if the user pushes back or asks unrelated questions.

# Running memory of earlier conversation
The conversation has earlier turns that are no longer shown verbatim. Here is a short summary of what was already discussed — use it for continuity, do not restate it back to the user unless relevant:

User previously asked about switching from a non-CS background into web development; was given a fundamentals-first roadmap (HTML/CSS/JS, build projects, consistency, don't rush frameworks).
```

No redaction was needed above — API keys are never part of the system prompt; they're injected separately via `createGoogleGenerativeAI({ apiKey })` at the provider level.

## Why the excerpts are framed as "style reference, not verbatim quotable"

The retrieved excerpts (section 5 above) are real tweets/blog snippets, but they're answers to *whatever the original tweet or post was about* — not necessarily the current user's question. If the model treated them as authoritative content to repeat, it would produce answers that are topically wrong (quoting a course-announcement tweet in response to a technical question) or would leak identifiable source text verbatim, which reads as scraped rather than "in character." The prompt explicitly instructs the model to use them only for tone/phrasing grounding, never as a direct answer source, and never to acknowledge that reference excerpts were provided at all — this keeps the illusion of character consistent rather than exposing the RAG mechanism to the user.

## Why the two personas needed genuinely distinct instructions

A shallow approach would template one persona and swap in a name and a few Hindi words for the other. The research notes (`lib/personas/notes.md`, "Notable contrast" section) surfaced real, sourced differences that made a shared template insufficient:

- **Hinglish density differs structurally, not just lexically.** Hitesh code-switches heavily and naturally in both speech and writing, anchored by one dominant verbal tic ("Hanji"). Piyush leans English-heavy in technical explanation — Hinglish appears, but his defining trait is *structural* (why-before-how), not a repeated word. Encoding both as "Hinglish teacher, tic = X" would have flattened Piyush's actual voice.
- **Source of authority differs.** Hitesh's tone rules and teaching approach lean on being a long-time educator/ex-CTO speaking about industry and hiring in general terms. Piyush's lean on being a current hands-on practitioner — his `teachingApproach` entries reference specific production mechanics (queues, WebRTC signaling, blocking the main thread) rather than career philosophy.
- **Rhetorical hook style differs.** Hitesh's sarcasm targets systems and culture (colleges, DSA-obsession, hype-chasing). Piyush's hooks are provocative *titles about the technology itself* ("X is Dead") that he then deliberately walks back — a self-aware bait-and-switch, not cultural criticism. This shows up in `piyush.json`'s `vocabularyQuirks` (`"X is Dead (provocative title framing, later walked back)"`) with no equivalent in Hitesh's spec.
- **Teaching structure differs.** Hitesh's `teachingApproach` centers on mindset (marathon, not sprint; "behind the scenes" understanding). Piyush's centers on a near-formulaic structure: scenario → problem/consequence → code → trade-off, which shows up almost verbatim across his blog posts and is encoded directly in his `teachingApproach` array.
- **Warmth vs. sharpness differs.** Hitesh's `doNots` and tone rules lean warm/mentor-friend even when blunt. Piyush's lean sharp/confident-practitioner — no evidence surfaced of a softer "friend" register comparable to Hitesh's, so none was invented.

Because `buildSystemPrompt` pulls every section directly from each persona's own JSON spec, these differences propagate automatically into every request — the two live sample conversations in [`docs/sample-conversations.md`](./sample-conversations.md) show the resulting voices are clearly distinguishable turn-to-turn, not just on the first reply.
